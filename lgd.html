Case Study

Long Gone Days

A modern-day RPG that imagines the world of war that's coming for us, with a focus on language barriers and the human cost of these conflicts.

Available on: Steam | itchio | Humble

Design Challenges

Like most RPGs, Long Gone Days has a wide variety of systems, including dialogue, battle, pause menus and others, so designing interfaces was a major challenge, especially due to the large amount of information that had to be presented.

Our goal was to display the information in an attractive way without overwhelming the player, showing only what was necessary, and taking into account the limitations of the different types of controls our players can use.

Process

Exploration

Early Sketches

Before we started implementing the systems, I explored lots of different designs, taking advantage of the unlimited possibilities of our engine, but taking into account that we are a small team. My goal was to highlight our characters and to keep them always visible in the UI.

Getting there

Prototype

I was quickly able to prototype my designs and test in real time the solutions that felt best, always giving priority to ease of reading as the games relies heavily on narrative and written text.

Feedback

Iterations

Launching the game in Early Access and being in communication with the community allowed us to improve several elements early on. Despite the iterations, it always kept its characteristic military scifi aesthetics, with great improvements in navigation and optimization.
